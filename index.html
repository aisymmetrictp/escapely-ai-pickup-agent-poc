Two order sources. Fickle partner stores. Customers who traveled to pick up and can't. The AI agent handles the full verification-first coordination loop — so no customer ever shows up to an empty store.

The real constraint — Tyler Perleberg, Escapely
"The last thing in the world we ever want to do is send a customer somewhere to pick up a box and have them not be able to get it. When we know right away that we can't fulfill the order with a pickup, the customer is much happier and more willing to get their game another way. When they get emails promising a game will be available and then it's not, they're furious."

The transformation
Today — manual, every weekend
6 steps, all human, all breakable
1
Customer books via website or Viator
2
Staff checks HubSpot — is pickup available in this city?
↳ manual lookup, no automation
3
Manually verify with store on the day they're going
↳ "oh we're randomly closed today"
↳ "we can't find the boxes"
↳ mom & pop stores, totally fickle
4
Tell store the customer's name (day-of only)
↳ this is when surprises happen
5
Tell customer where to go and what hours
6
Update HubSpot: pickup date, name, reduce inventory
↳ often forgotten on busy weekends
With AI Agent — verify-first
Agent runs the loop. Store confirmed before customer notified.
1
Order arrives from website or Viator webhook
2
Agent queries HubSpot — city has pickup? Stock available?
↳ instant. No human needed.
✓
Day-of: Agent contacts store to verify open + has stock
↳ if store can't fulfill → immediately pivots to shipping
↳ customer never promised anything until confirmed
4
Store notified with customer name + pickup window
5
Customer notified only after store confirms
↳ "your box is ready at X, come between Y–Z"
6
HubSpot updated automatically: date, name, inventory
↳ on handoff confirmation from store
